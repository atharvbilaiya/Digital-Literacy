# Case Study — UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud
**Category:** Financial Cybercrime

---

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is basically when someone tricks you into either approving a fake payment request or giving away sensitive info like your OTP or PIN — usually through some form of manipulation or a fake interface designed to look legit.

## How It Typically Happens — Step by Step

1. **Target Selection:** The fraudster looks for a potential victim — often someone who's selling something on OLX or Quikr, or just random people they call while pretending to be from a bank or government office.

2. **Initial Contact:** They reach out posing as a buyer, a bank employee, or a customer support agent. They're usually pretty convincing — they speak professionally, and sometimes they already have some of your personal details which makes them seem trustworthy.

3. **The Deception:** This is where it happens — they either send you a fake "collect money" UPI request (which looks almost identical to a "send money" screen), or they convince you over the phone to share your UPI PIN or OTP, saying it's needed to "confirm" or "receive" your payment.

4. **The Transaction:** The second you approve that request or read out your OTP, the money is gone. It gets debited from your account instantly.

5. **Disappearance:** The fraudster cuts the call immediately and the money gets bounced through multiple accounts very quickly, which makes it really hard to trace or recover.

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users — students and elderly people especially
- Anyone selling stuff on second-hand platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace
- People who recently applied for jobs (there's a common scam where you're asked to pay a "registration fee")
- Anyone whose phone number is publicly visible somewhere

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss — usually somewhere between ₹5,000 and ₹2,00,000
- A lot of stress and loss of trust in digital payments in general
- It's very difficult to get the money back once it's been transferred
- In some cases, the victim's account gets used as a "money mule" by the fraudster, which can create legal problems for the victim themselves
