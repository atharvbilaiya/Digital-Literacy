# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## 🔐 General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. At least 8 characters, mix of uppercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., `Vit@2024!`). If remembering them all is a problem, use a password manager.

- [ ] **2. Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** for all important accounts — Gmail, GitHub, LinkedIn, banking apps. Even if someone gets your password, they still can't get in without the second step.

- [ ] **3. Don't click on random links** you get through SMS, WhatsApp, email, or social media — even if it's from someone you know, their account might have been compromised.

- [ ] **4. Keep your apps and phone OS updated.** Those update notifications aren't just annoying pop-ups — they fix security gaps that hackers actively look for. Turn on auto-updates if you can.

- [ ] **5. Be careful with public Wi-Fi.** Don't log into your bank or anything sensitive on free/public networks. If you have to, use a VPN.

- [ ] **6. Check what permissions your apps actually have.** A torch app doesn't need access to your contacts. A calculator doesn't need your location. Go through your phone settings and clean this up.

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **7. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or ATM PIN with anyone.** Not family, not "bank staff", not anyone claiming to be from a government office. Actual banks and NPCI will never ask for your PIN — ever.

- [ ] **8. Know the difference between sending and receiving money on UPI.** You do NOT enter your PIN to receive money. If someone tells you to enter your PIN to get a payment, that's a scam. Hang up and block them.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **9. Set your personal Instagram and Facebook accounts to Private.** Only let people you actually know follow you.

- [ ] **10. Search your own name online once in a while.** See what comes up and whether anything sensitive is publicly visible — and take steps to remove or hide it if needed.

---

## 🆘 If Something Happens — Report It Fast

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (24×7, toll-free) |
| **Online Reporting Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest cyber cell |

> ⚠️ **Report within the first hour** if you've been scammed financially — the quicker you report, the better the chance of getting the money frozen before it disappears.
