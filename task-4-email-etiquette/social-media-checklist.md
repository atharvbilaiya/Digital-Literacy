# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Do** keep your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles looking professional — proper photo, decent bio | Recruiters and professors actually check these |
| 2 | **Do** think before you post — quick test: would you be okay if your college or a future employer saw it? | Screenshots exist. Deleted posts don't actually disappear |
| 3 | **Do** use privacy settings to limit who can see your personal stuff on Instagram, Facebook, etc. | Keeps your personal life separate from professional contacts |
| 4 | **Do** give credit when you share someone else's work, ideas, or images | It's just the right thing to do — and avoids plagiarism issues |
| 5 | **Do** report and block accounts that bully or harass you | Cyberbullying is actually a criminal offence under India's IT Act |
| 6 | **Do** use your college email when signing up for academic platforms | Keeps things organised and separates work accounts from personal ones |
| 7 | **Do** verify information before sharing it — especially news or anything health-related | Sharing misinformation can have real consequences, even if unintentional |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It's Dangerous |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't** share your OTP, password, or PIN with anyone — even if they say they're from your bank | This is literally how most UPI fraud happens |
| 2 | **Don't** post your home address, phone number, or daily routine publicly | That kind of info can easily be misused |
| 3 | **Don't** accept follow/friend requests from strangers without checking their profile first | Fake accounts are commonly used for phishing and scams |
| 4 | **Don't** post offensive, hateful, or discriminatory content — even if you think it's a joke | It can lead to disciplinary action at college and even legal trouble |
| 5 | **Don't** reuse the same password across different platforms | One hacked account means all your accounts are at risk |
| 6 | **Don't** click on links from unknown accounts claiming you've won something or offering a job | Almost always a phishing or scam link |
| 7 | **Don't** air out conflicts with classmates, professors, or your college publicly on social media | It creates a permanent record and usually just makes things worse for everyone |
